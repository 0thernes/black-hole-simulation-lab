## Black-hole shadows / Kerr spin / EHT  *(directly validates the renderer)*

- **Adaptive ray tracing, image diagnostics, and photon ring signatures of
  rotating dark-matter-dressed black holes** — M. Fathi, 2026, *arXiv preprint*.
  Backward-ray-traces two effective rotating backgrounds grown from static
  dark-matter-sourced seed metrics (a regular Einasto-type hole and a cored-NFW
  hole) against Kerr as reference, building observer-screen images, lensing
  bands, transfer maps, and synthetic intensities under a common semi-analytic
  accretion prescription; the Einasto geometry stays very close to Kerr while
  the cored-NFW case redistributes the image — larger centroid displacement,
  stronger brightness asymmetry, an outward bright-ring shift, and a changed
  visibility amplitude — deviations partly degenerate with spin, inclination,
  and emission modeling. *tier: numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/020b7f7372da5381bb7445bc0f7daf09/
- **Beyond Sgr A* and M87*: Sub-Microarcsecond Black Hole Shadow Detection via
  Lunar-based Extremely Long Baseline Interferometry** — S. Zhao et al., 2026,
  *arXiv preprint*. Assesses how a lunar-based 230 GHz VLBI station (≈0.85 µas
  resolution, motivated by LOVEX/QueQiao-2 and the planned ILRS) would extend
  black-hole-shadow imaging beyond the only two current EHT targets; simulating
  Moon–Earth (u,v) coverage for 31 large-angular-size supermassive black holes,
  it finds source geometry relative to the Moon's orbit sets whether the
  first-visibility-null shadow indicator is sampled, and flags M104, NGC 5077,
  and NGC 1052 as detectable with a 5 m dish, with photon-ring access for
  Sgr A*/M87*/NGC 1600/M31 given space-telescope infill. *tier:
  numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/9a92a7714b04591b9cff1af1033dc28f/

## Numerical relativity / ringdown / QNM / GW modeling  *(adjacent observational GR)*

- **Quasinormal Mode Content of Binary Black Hole Ringdowns** — R. Dyer et al.,
  2026, *Physical Review Letters*. A fully Bayesian, data-driven pipeline
  identifies quasinormal modes in high-accuracy Cauchy-characteristic-evolution
  NR waveforms from a public catalog, extracting overtones, retrograde modes,
  and nonlinear modes up to cubic order; multiple high-order overtones appear
  near merger (confirming their modeling relevance), the mode content is
  tabulated across ringdown start times as a systematic reference, and — as
  expected for CCE waveforms — late-time power-law tails are absent. *tier:
  numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/468c9fe1ab0e577684ce8180dc5ce2a2/
- **Advancing the effective-one-body framework in the test-mass limit
  (SEOB-TML)** — N. Nishimura et al., 2026, *Physical Review D*. An enhanced EOB
  model for quasi-circular spin-aligned binaries in the test-mass limit:
  a quadrupole-factorized flux prescription (including horizon absorption) maps
  higher multipoles onto a single (2,2) baseline to slash fractional flux
  errors, a phenomenological ansatz replaces next-to-quasicircular corrections
  for a smooth merger transition, and qnmfinder-extracted QNM coefficients model
  mode-mixing (plus a full-waveform (2,0) mode); it markedly reduces dephasing
  versus SEOBNRv5HM, especially for retrograde configurations. *tier:
  numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/ed497be7ce1355e196abd0c3355db3e2/
- **Gravitational Memory from Hairy Binary Black Hole Mergers** — S. Gasparotto
  et al., 2026, *arXiv preprint*. First computation of gravitational-wave (null)
  memory from full inspiral–merger–ringdown waveforms in a theory beyond GR,
  using scalar-Gauss-Bonnet gravity: derives spin-weighted-harmonic tensor-
  memory expressions from the Horndeski memory formulas and evaluates them on
  existing shift-symmetric and dynamically-scalarizing NR waveforms; the
  dominant effect is an indirect modification through altered nonlinear merger
  dynamics (direct scalar contribution suppressed), giving up to ~4% memory-
  amplitude deviation from GR and boosting the GR/sGB waveform mismatch by over
  an order of magnitude — complementary information for third-generation
  detector tests, especially low-mass binaries. *tier: numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/ba74e09ef0ed5f3c9b870af9faae60dc/

## Quantum gravity / information paradox / holography  *(speculative context — label, do not let it touch the kernel)*

- **Causality Criteria for Island Models** — F. Deng, 2026, *arXiv preprint*.
  Clarifies why effective island descriptions in double holography can appear to
  violate micro-causality even though the bulk is local and causal, arguing the
  apparent non-causality is a mismatch between effective spacetime separation and
  bulk causal accessibility rather than a feature of nonlocal reconstruction
  (which merely encodes quantum error correction in a restricted code subspace);
  it proposes a three-part structural criterion — no extra propagation channels,
  a local bulk-supported operator dictionary, and consistent effective/bulk
  causal matching — and shows brane-world island realizations (including the
  defect-extremal-surface construction) satisfy it, including during island
  formation and evaporation. *tier: speculative_extension.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/3512ba3666be5705a1d059ccb38d036e/
- **Black Hole Interior and Quantum Error Correction with Dynamical Gravity** —
  A. Miyata et al., 2026, *arXiv preprint*. Revisits the causality worry that
  island-formula embedding of interior code-subspace information into Hawking
  radiation could let operations on the radiation alter the interior: whereas
  earlier PSSY-model (topological-gravity-only) analyses found large errors
  yield a causality-violating nonzero mutual information, allowing dynamical
  gravity to also act on the radiation induces a bulk-geometry backreaction in
  the path integral that drives the relevant mutual information to zero,
  resolving the apparent violation. *tier: speculative_extension.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/074b51c25b5150d695ad1df49294fce0/
- **Microscopic Unitarity and the Quantization of Black Hole Evaporation Time** —
  A. Abutaleb, 2026, *arXiv preprint*. Credible-but-fringe: builds an effective
  microscopic time-dependent-Hamiltonian model of evaporation under an
  "Independent Unitary Pairing" assumption (the Hilbert space split into mutually
  independent interior-qubit/radiation-qubit bipartite subsystems, each evolving
  unitarily) and a "Fermion-like Occupancy Bound" holographic capacity
  constraint (ln 2 max entropy per channel) to recover the entropy turnaround
  and final-state purification, yielding a quantization condition for unitarity
  that couples microscopic phase evolution to macroscopic observables. *tier:
  speculative_extension.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/f5ab80b4866a55c8a8d7c728aae340bf/
