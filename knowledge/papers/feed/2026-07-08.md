## Black-hole shadows / Kerr spin / EHT  *(directly validates the renderer)*

- **Black Hole Polarimetry. II. The Connection between Spin and Polarization** —
  G. N. Wong et al., 2026, *The Astrophysical Journal*. Shows the resolved
  synchrotron-polarization observable ∠β₂ in horizon-scale images depends
  strongly and systematically on black-hole spin via the spin–magnetic-field
  coupling (analytic in the force-free limit, extended by a semi-analytic
  equatorial GRMHD inflow model); applied to EHT data it disfavors high-spin
  configurations for both M87* and Sgr A*, making polarimetric imaging a spin
  diagnostic. *tier: observational_constraint.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/d6c933560e3c5f2a86aade51cdc67564/
- **Testing loop quantum gravity through EHT observations of M87* and Sgr A*
  using rotating holonomy-corrected black holes** — H. Ali et al., 2026, *arXiv
  preprint*. Uses EHT shadow-area and oblateness observables to constrain the LQG
  holonomy-correction parameter b of a rotating holonomy-corrected metric: b
  enlarges the shadow and pushes prograde photon orbits outward, closed photon
  rings survive even without a horizon, and current M87*/Sgr A* angular-diameter
  bounds admit nonzero b (e.g. b ≤ 0.42 M at a = 0.784 M for M87*), leaving
  RHCBHs viable Kerr alternatives. *tier: observational_constraint.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/831f23b668915ba6a70bd561b592d285/
- **Optical images of Kerr-Sen black hole illuminated by thick accretion disks** —
  Y.-K. Wang et al., 2026, *arXiv preprint*. Radiative-transfer ray tracing of a
  Kerr-Sen (dilaton-charge) black hole under geometrically-thick, optically-thin
  RIAF and ballistic (BAAF) accretion models: increasing charge Q shrinks the
  photon rings and central dark region, frame dragging drives a spin- and
  inclination-dependent brightness asymmetry, and Q compresses/rotates the
  photon-ring EVPA structure in the polarization maps. *tier:
  numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/a3f32281c8bb5621b4cae2ee2de464a6/

## Numerical relativity / ringdown / QNM / GW modeling  *(adjacent observational GR)*

- **Fast frequency-domain phenomenological modeling of eccentric aligned-spin
  binary black holes (IMRPhenomXE)** — A. Ramos-Buades et al., 2026, *Physical
  Review D*. Extends the quasi-circular IMRPhenomXAS (2,2) model to elliptical
  orbits via orbit-averaged 3PN quasi-Keplerian dynamics and a stationary-phase
  waveform expanded to O(e¹²), assuming circularization at merger-ringdown;
  validated against 186 SXS NR waveforms it stays below 3% unfaithfulness for
  72% of runs with e < 0.4 and outruns every existing eccentric IMR model in
  speed, making it a ready-to-use catalog-era approximant. *tier:
  numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/48f13fe3561953018409275cf8c126bb/
- **Direct Waves in Black-Hole Binary Mergers: Insights from the Backwards One
  Body Model** — A. Kankani et al., 2026, *arXiv preprint*. Isolates a non-QNM
  "direct wave" component of merger-ringdown radiation (prompt emission from the
  plunging perturber) using rational filters on BOB and NR waveforms: recovers
  the BOB amplitude evolution from Pöschl–Teller QNM pole contributions, shows
  BOB naturally captures the direct wave (explaining its accuracy near the
  waveform peak), and finds the direct-wave frequency tracks the peak-News
  frequency rather than the horizon frequency even at high remnant spin. *tier:
  numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/c75044cfb42c5d0fb56b9cf4d9feb428/
- **Highly eccentric non-spinning binary black hole mergers: quadrupolar
  post-merger waveforms** — N. Rao et al., 2026, *arXiv preprint*. Builds
  numerically-informed closed-form (2,2) post-merger waveforms from 233
  non-spinning eccentric RIT-catalog simulations, constructing time-dependent
  complex QNM amplitudes via a Bayesian procedure and multivariate polynomial
  fits in the symmetric mass ratio and the merger-evaluated effective energy and
  angular momentum; cross-validated on SXS it reaches ~10⁻³ mismatches even at
  near-extreme eccentricity and slots onto EOB/phenomenological inspirals for
  full IMR templates. *tier: numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/259831acd2f95f7285685a75bf5a90df/

## Quantum gravity / information paradox / holography  *(speculative context — label, do not let it touch the kernel)*

- **Entanglement islands, Page curves and phase transitions of Kerr-AdS black
  holes** — D. Das et al., 2026, *The European Physical Journal C*. Applies the
  island rule to a rotating Kerr-AdS black hole, recovering a Page curve
  (early-time linear growth, late-time saturation) that never violates unitarity,
  and — the novel part — studies how black-hole phase transitions imprint on it
  across two ensembles, finding a first-order transition produces a sharp
  discontinuity in the Page curve. *tier: speculative_extension.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/763d3eb481ec5f6a9619313f9de3d6f2/
- **Thermodynamics and information recovery of Schwarzschild AdS black holes in
  conformal Killing gravity** — Y. Ladghami et al., 2026, *The European Physical
  Journal C*. In conformal Killing gravity (cosmological constant as pressure,
  the CKG parameter as an independent variable) the Bekenstein–Hawking area law
  survives while the CKG parameter reshapes the phase structure (Van der
  Waals-like criticality for positive sign, none for negative); the island
  prescription restores the Page curve after Page time, saturating at twice the
  Bekenstein–Hawking entropy, with Page time expressible in thermodynamic
  quantities. *tier: speculative_extension.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/6618ee23e1ef55a69dee39122e70ae10/
- **Entanglement islands of 2D charged scalar-hairy black holes on the brane** —
  Q. Li et al., 2026, *arXiv preprint*. Computes the island-formula entanglement
  entropy and Page curve of a 2D brane Maxwell-scalar black hole coupled to
  thermal baths in double holography: injecting electric charge raises the
  saturation entropy (more brane degrees of freedom via higher effective global
  tension) while the scalar source suppresses it, yet the pre-saturation
  dynamics stay robust (early quadratic → late linear growth), with Page time
  set by a competition between thermal excitation and brane DOF. *tier:
  speculative_extension.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/e3dc14f1a4675c0d8cedbb99e140b0de/
