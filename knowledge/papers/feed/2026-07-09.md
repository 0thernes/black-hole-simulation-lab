## Black-hole shadows / Kerr spin / EHT  *(directly validates the renderer)*

- **Impact of Perfect Fluid Dark Matter on the Appearance of Rotating Black
  Hole** — Y.-X. Huang et al., 2026, *Physics of the Dark Universe*. High-res
  general-relativistic ray-tracing of thin-disk images at 87/230 GHz for a
  rotating black hole in perfect-fluid dark matter: ISCO existence jointly
  constrains the DM intensity k and spin a, and folding in EHT angular-diameter
  measurements narrows the viable space — PFDM improves agreement with M87*'s
  compact shadow and asymmetric brightness. *tier: numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/8255719daced5f0d875f82ab9a1287b9/
- **Photon regions, shadow observables and constraints from M87* of a
  Kerr-Newman-like black hole in Bumblebee gravity surrounded by plasma** —
  J. Zhang et al., 2026, *arXiv preprint*. Separable null geodesics in a
  power-law plasma around a Lorentz-violating charged rotating black hole; spin a
  and the LV parameter ℓ drive shadow distortion while charge Q₀ and plasma k
  shrink it radially. Modeling M87* as this object, the EHT angular diameter
  θ_d = 42±3 μas and ΔC ≲ 0.1 keep it a viable candidate. *tier:
  observational_constraint.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/eda1682ef2ef50f391da0fc5e0217eb6/
- **Circular orbits and observational features of the rotating Simpson-Visser
  black hole surrounded by a thin accretion disk** — Z. Li et al., 2026, *Chinese
  Physics C*. Novikov-Thorne thin-disk radiative properties and backward
  ray-traced images of a rotating Simpson-Visser (regular) Kerr-mimicker:
  radiative efficiency matches Kerr exactly, but the regularization parameter g
  suppresses flux/temperature/luminosity and widens the photon-ring region,
  offering an accretion-based route to break the shadow-size degeneracy with
  Kerr. *tier: numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/a7da21d9a78b59c9aa5b611ec3829843/
- **Rotating Black Holes with Primary Scalar Hair: Shadow Signatures in Beyond
  Horndeski Gravity** — K. Nozari et al., 2026, *arXiv preprint*. Photon regions
  and shadows of a rotating black hole carrying primary scalar hair Q: negative Q
  enlarges the shadow and lowers oblateness, positive Q shrinks and distorts it;
  imposing the M87* bounds θ_d = 42±3 μas and ΔC ≤ 0.1 leaves a viable (a,Q)
  region (tightly restricted for Q>0), with deviations at the O(μas) edge of
  present sensitivity. *tier: observational_constraint.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/59c3678fb6f4547a818f28e7bdb86684/

## Numerical relativity / ringdown / QNM / GW modeling  *(adjacent observational GR)*

- **Novel ringdown tests of general relativity with black hole greybody
  factors** — R. F. Rosato et al., 2026, *arXiv preprint*. GreyRing models the
  post-merger signal from the remnant's greybody factor, reproducing the
  frequency-domain (2,2) ringdown of aligned-spin NR waveforms to O(10⁻⁶)
  mismatch and outperforming time-domain models; it enables an agnostic
  strong-gravity consistency test (greybody-inferred vs spectroscopy mass/spin)
  that needs no overtones or early start times, validated on GW250114. *tier:
  numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/1e675530c91d586c8bb9ee5d2f06a46e/
- **Ringdown tests of general relativity with spin-precession** — E. Maggio,
  2026, *Journal of Physics: Conference Series*. A parametrized QNM test that
  accounts for spin precession: neglecting precession can fake GR deviations even
  at current sensitivity. Reanalyzing GWTC-3 and hierarchically combining events,
  it constrains fractional deviations in the dominant-mode frequency and damping
  time. *tier: observational_constraint.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/ecfe89f95f0351ec857fb20e63c0c9f6/
- **Advancing the effective-one-body framework in the test-mass limit
  (SEOB-TML)** — N. Nishimura et al., 2026, *Physical Review D*. Enhanced EOB
  model for quasi-circular spin-aligned binaries in the test-mass limit: a
  quadrupole-factorized flux prescription (including horizon absorption) captures
  higher multipoles without explicit mode sums, a phenomenological attachment
  replaces NQC corrections, and qnmfinder QNM coefficients model mode mixing —
  cutting residuals versus SEOBNRv5HM, especially for retrograde configurations.
  *tier: numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/ed497be7ce1355e196abd0c3355db3e2/

## Quantum gravity / information paradox / holography  *(speculative context — label, do not let it touch the kernel)*

- **Real-time entanglement dynamics and information flux near black-hole
  horizons** — F. Neukart, 2026, *Journal of Physics A: Mathematical and
  Theoretical*. An effective finite-capacity spacetime where near-horizon regions
  act as quantum memory registers coupled unitarily to field modes: the
  macroscopic limit reproduces the Bekenstein–Hawking entropy balance, while
  finite capacity adds small unitary corrections; exact small-system simulations
  show Page-curve-like radiation entropy, echo-type modulations, and mutual-
  information revivals. *tier: speculative_extension.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/fe9ef07d8e6e5670b12bc346b104d07f/
- **Evaporating Black Hole Interior and Complexity Evolution** — N. Bragagnolo
  et al., 2026, *arXiv preprint*. In JT gravity with an end-of-the-world brane
  entangled to a radiation system, a boundary-to-brane geodesic length is read as
  subsystem complexity: including spacetime and replica wormholes, the evaporating
  interior's complexity grows linearly, peaks at Page time ~O(S_BH), then decays
  exponentially — and loses self-averaging as fluctuations grow past Page time,
  unlike the eternal-black-hole plateau. *tier: speculative_extension.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/715a1ed7f674524c91fdc65e2f1d8af9/
- **Relational quantum dynamics of the black hole interior: singularity
  resolution and quantum bounce** — P. Fragolino et al., 2026, *arXiv preprint*.
  Fully relational, gauge-invariant quantization of the Schwarzschild interior
  (Kantowski-Sachs) via refined algebraic quantization and a Page-Wootters clock:
  the Kretschmann and expansion scalars stay finite, the 2-sphere area is bounded
  below, and the expansion scalar changes sign at a quantum bounce — a
  black-hole-to-white-hole transition with singularity resolution from
  relationality and uncertainty alone. *tier: speculative_extension.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/d5ca27d081db52aab741f81f3b1bfdc8/
