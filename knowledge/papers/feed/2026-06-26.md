## Black-hole shadows / Kerr spin / EHT  *(directly validates the renderer)*

- **Optical images of a Kerr–Sen black hole illuminated by thick accretion
  disks** — Y.-K. Wang et al., 2026, *arXiv preprint*. RIAF + ballistic
  thick-disk ray tracing of a Kerr–Sen black hole; dilaton charge Q shrinks the
  photon rings and central dark region together, while spin and inclination
  drive a frame-dragging brightness asymmetry, with accompanying polarization
  maps. *tier: numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/a3f32281c8bb5621b4cae2ee2de464a6/
- **Probing rotating Hayward black holes: magnetic-charge distortions, dynamic
  shadows, and polarization patterns** — P. Wang et al., 2026, *Chinese Physics
  C*. Ray-traced images of a regular (Hayward) rotating black hole; magnetic
  charge induces a "D-shaped" shadow distortion and enhanced photon-ring
  polarization that are observably distinct from Kerr. *tier:
  numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/19e666fac7a259afa0d1f2639b0f8b99/
- **The Role of Interferometric Phase in Measuring Black Hole Photon Rings** —
  S. Gutiérrez-Lara et al., 2026, *arXiv preprint*. Synthetic EHT/BHEX VLBI of
  Kerr multi-ring images; interferometric phase tracks photon-ring displacement
  (~1 µas per unit dimensionless spin → ~44° on a 25 Gλ baseline for an M87*-like
  model), giving a forward-modeled spin diagnostic. *tier:
  numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/ec5f8d91f251549a8531fa5e2e716936/

## Numerical relativity / ringdown / QNM  *(adjacent observational GR)*

- **A nonlinear voice from GW250114 ringdown** — Y.-F. Wang et al., 2026, *arXiv
  preprint*. Reports first observational evidence of quadratic (nonlinear) QNMs
  in a real ringdown: six modes from quadratic coupling of the (2,2,0) mode and
  its first two overtones in GW250114, with zero amplitude excluded at 3.0σ and
  the measured amplitudes consistent with theory. *tier:
  observational_constraint.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/4fea862c7abd56cdb8f89fa8c9647d40/
- **Direct Waves in Black-Hole Binary Mergers: Insights from the Backwards One
  Body Model** — A. Kankani et al., 2026, *arXiv preprint*. Shows the BOB
  waveform naturally captures a non-QNM "direct wave" from the plunging
  perturber, explaining its near-peak accuracy; the direct-wave frequency tracks
  the News at peak amplitude rather than the horizon frequency, even at high
  spin. *tier: numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/c75044cfb42c5d0fb56b9cf4d9feb428/
- **Fast frequency-domain phenomenological modeling of eccentric aligned-spin
  binary black holes (IMRPhenomXE)** — A. Ramos-Buades et al., 2026, *Phys. Rev.
  D*. Eccentric extension of IMRPhenomXAS (e ≲ 0.8); under 3% unfaithfulness for
  72% of SXS numerical-relativity runs at e < 0.4 and faster than existing
  eccentric inspiral-merger-ringdown models. *tier: numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/48f13fe3561953018409275cf8c126bb/

## Quantum gravity / information paradox  *(speculative context — label, do not let it touch the kernel)*

- **Island of an acoustic black hole in Schwarzschild spacetime** — (author list
  n/a in index), 2026, *Phys. Rev. D*. Applies the island formula to analog
  Hawking phonons of a superfluid acoustic horizon; recovers a Page curve
  (unitarity) in the nonextremal case, with a divergent entropy and ill-defined
  Page time in the extremal limit. *tier: speculative_extension.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/23e11db4a1ff5f66913049272cec4bbd/
- **Entanglement islands, Page curves and phase transitions of Kerr-AdS black
  holes** — D. Das et al., 2026, *Eur. Phys. J. C*. Island-rule entanglement
  entropy for a Kerr-AdS black hole; a first-order phase transition leaves a
  sharp discontinuity in the Page curve while preserving unitarity across
  ensembles. *tier: speculative_extension.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/763d3eb481ec5f6a9619313f9de3d6f2/
- **Seeing Page Curves and Islands with Blinders On** — H. Geng et al., 2026,
  *arXiv preprint*. Argues the bulk Hilbert space does not factorize along the
  radial direction, so islands/Page curves describe redistribution between
  observed and unobserved degrees of freedom rather than true information
  recovery; replies to arXiv:2506.04311. *tier: speculative_extension.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/e0cf95ddee2855b3a7437c604f52ff1a/
