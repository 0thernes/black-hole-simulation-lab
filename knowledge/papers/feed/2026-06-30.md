## Black-hole shadows / Kerr spin / EHT  *(directly validates the renderer)*

- **Ellis–Bronnikov Wormhole Shadows with Spherically Symmetric Accretion Flow** —
  M. M. Takahashi et al., 2026, *arXiv preprint*. General-relativistic radiative
  transfer (GRRT) of a steady spherical accretion flow with synchrotron emission;
  the horizonless Ellis–Bronnikov wormhole shows a central shadow + bright photon
  ring like Schwarzschild, but both are brighter because matter near and beyond
  the throat also radiates — and both still sit within current EHT M87* error
  bars. *tier: numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/a32e7df307395d8f9d464067a5916594/
- **Strong Gravitational Lensing and Shadow Signatures of Anisotropic Black Holes
  in Plasma** — P. Yovkochev et al., 2026, *Chinese Physics C*. Static spherical
  spacetime sourced by anisotropic matter with exponentially decaying hair in a
  non-magnetized plasma; deformation parameters ṽ₂ (shrinks) and ṽ_c (enlarges)
  push the photon sphere and shadow in opposite directions, plasma inflates the
  shadow, and EHT Sgr A* data constrain the parameter space. *tier:
  observational_constraint.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/c6368e2b92545a10bcdef12ada6d8b07/
- **Detectability of secondary images from flares near Sgr A* with mock GRAVITY
  data** — F. Xie et al., 2026, *arXiv preprint*. Bayesian forecast of whether the
  flux-weighted centroid of NIR flares orbiting Sgr A* reveals strong-lensed
  secondary images; robust detection (|ΔBIC|>7.9) needs both ~10× more flares and
  astrometric errors cut to ~40% of current GRAVITY values — a stepping stone
  toward resolving higher-order images and photon rings. *tier:
  numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/040c52cdb24c559da2e0e76bd2cb8e6d/

## Numerical relativity / ringdown / QNM  *(adjacent observational GR)*

- **Measuring a Black Hole's Area Immediately after Merger: A Direct-Wave Test of
  Hawking's Area Law** — A. K.-W. Chung et al., 2026, *arXiv preprint*. Infers a
  Kerr-equivalent horizon area from the near-merger "direct wave" (before
  quasinormal ringing dominates); applied to GW250114, windows initiated 3–4.5 M
  before peak amplitude give an area consistent with the Kerr remnant — a first
  direct-wave area measurement and a new near-merger area-law test. *tier:
  observational_constraint.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/428fe4cee3c159339412d799a311af9b/
- **Spectral suppression of black hole ringdown tails** — J. A. León Vega et al.,
  2026, *arXiv preprint*. Explains why Price's-law late-time power-law tails are
  largely absent in quasi-circular binary-merger NR waveforms: an oscillatory
  source of carrier frequency ν and width σ suppresses the branch-cut excitation
  by α = σν (Gaussian pulse → ~e^{−α²/2}), confirmed by Regge–Wheeler evolutions
  and predicting tail enhancement in head-on / eccentric mergers. *tier:
  numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/64c0457ca9cb5b278496884ca7468bb5/
- **The gravitational wave–black hole imaging correspondence for modified black
  holes** — D. Díaz-Guerra et al., 2026, *arXiv preprint*. Tests the eikonal
  correspondence linking ringdown QNM real/imaginary parts to the photon-region
  critical impact parameter and Lyapunov exponent across many modified spherical
  BH geometries; the relation stays surprisingly accurate even at low multipole ℓ,
  motivating joint imaging + ringdown tests. *tier: numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/dfe8679f71935fb98a9c8197d4bf7ffd/

## Quantum gravity / information paradox / holography  *(speculative context — label, do not let it touch the kernel)*

- **Smooth horizons from topology change in canonical quantum gravity** —
  V. Chandrasekaran, 2026, *arXiv preprint*. Resolves the JT-gravity firewall
  paradox by adding topology change ("pair of pants") to canonical quantization
  under relational time; horizon boost edge modes dress the Hawking modes, the
  firewall branch is annihilated by the gravitational constraints after the Page
  time, and horizon-vacuum and early-radiation-purity become the same Dirac
  observable. *tier: speculative_extension.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/6b2023d4d0e0515f99a490c38ec5e504/
- **Holographic affinity: Weak gravity conjecture favors CFT thermodynamics in
  RN-AdS black holes with perfect fluid dark matter** — Hoang Van Quyet, 2026,
  *Annals of Physics*. Compares restricted-phase-space vs CFT thermodynamics for
  RN-AdS black holes in perfect-fluid dark matter; the weak-gravity and weak-
  cosmic-censorship conjectures are jointly satisfiable, with the holographic
  boundary (CFT) description giving a wider compatibility window (3ℓ<γ<10ℓ) and
  acting as a natural Swampland filter. *tier: speculative_extension.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/d2449cc8c1a959d998f3e927669fa2fb/
- **Holographic complexity of de-Sitter black holes** — C. Fang et al., 2026,
  *arXiv preprint*. Evaluates Complexity = Volume / Spacetime-Volume / Action for
  Schwarzschild–de Sitter under both static-patch and dS/CFT prescriptions; CV and
  CV2.0 grow linearly at late time while CA growth vanishes (finite regularized
  WDW action), and the static-patch and dS/CFT growth rates coincide — hinting at
  a unified de Sitter bulk description. *tier: speculative_extension.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/e57ef4013c145edd8d804166b96c9eae/
