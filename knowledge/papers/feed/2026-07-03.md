## Black-hole shadows / Kerr spin / EHT  *(directly validates the renderer)*

- **Probing Kalb–Ramond gravity with charged rotating black holes: constraints
  from EHT observations** — T. A. Nengroo et al., 2026, *Physics of the Dark
  Universe*. Charged rotating black holes in Kalb–Ramond (string-motivated,
  spontaneously Lorentz-violating) gravity, with a radial mass function set by a
  Lorentz-violating parameter ℓ and charge Q; the shadow radius is suppressed by
  √(1−ℓ) while charge adds distortion, and M87* (θ_o=17°) and Sgr A* (θ_o=50°)
  angular-diameter windows carve out the viable (ℓ, Q, a) region, giving an upper
  bound ℓ≲0.19 from Sgr A*. *tier: observational_constraint.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/17a489dbb28f5fcab5bee98fcb642f03/
- **Measuring the Black Hole and Accretion Parameters of Sagittarius A* from EHT
  Observations using a Semi-Analytic Model** — B. J. Marazzo-Nowicki et al.,
  2026, *arXiv preprint*. Bayesian snapshot fitting of a semi-analytic emission
  model (jointly with station gains) to real 2017 EHT Sgr A* visibilities, with
  hierarchical stacking to separate persistent strong-gravity structure from
  intrinsic variability; spin and magnetic-field parameters stay poorly
  constrained by the 2017 data, but it favors a near-face-on inclination
  (θ_o≈9.2°) and an emission peak near the horizon (R_peak≈4.9 GM/c²). *tier:
  observational_constraint.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/678cadd2ae465278883f12f018437a8c/
- **BHCast: Unlocking Black Hole Plasma Dynamics from a Single Blurry Image with
  Long-Term Forecasting** — R. Tu et al., 2026, *arXiv preprint*. A neural
  framework that forecasts accretion-plasma dynamics from a single EHT-resolution
  snapshot — autoregressively super-resolving the blurry frame into a stable
  high-resolution movie, extracting pattern speed and pitch angle, then
  regressing spin and viewing inclination with gradient-boosted trees; validated
  on Sgr A*/M87* simulations blurred to EHT resolution and on real EHT M87*
  images. *tier: numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/7bb0a478850f5b3b84059c128175ad19/

## Numerical relativity / ringdown / QNM / GW modeling  *(adjacent observational GR)*

- **Searching for extreme-mass-ratio inspirals in LISA: From identification to
  parameter estimation** — (author list n/a in index), 2026, *Physical Review
  D*. Presents an end-to-end LISA extreme-mass-ratio-inspiral analysis pipeline
  spanning signal identification through full parameter estimation. *tier:
  numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/941594ab09dd56f393c5f3ec807314a9/
- **Probing Quantum Gravity effects with Extreme Mass Ratio Inspirals around
  Rotating Hayward Black Holes** — D. Zhang et al., 2026, *arXiv preprint*.
  EMRIs into a rotating Hayward (regular) black hole, where the quantum parameter
  α₀ adds correction terms to both the orbital frequency and the radiated fluxes;
  waveforms generated via the augmented-analytic-kludge FastEMRIWaveforms model
  with time-delay interferometry and a Fisher-matrix analysis show α₀ induces a
  detectable one-year LISA waveform dephasing. *tier: numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/1ca2b4a80d1355d49db958a8601cd3bb/
- **Probing Kerr Symmetry Breaking with LISA Extreme-Mass-Ratio Inspirals** —
  P. F. Muguruza et al., 2026, *arXiv preprint*. EMRIs around generic non-Kerr
  compact objects carrying both axisymmetric and non-axisymmetric mass quadrupole
  and octupole moments (parameterizing the breaking of two Kerr symmetries); a
  Fisher analysis of a one-year 10 M_⊙→10⁶ M_⊙ inspiral at SNR 30 finds LISA
  could bound equatorial-symmetry breaking to ~10⁻² and axial-symmetry breaking
  to ~10⁻³, testing horizon-scale (e.g. fuzzball) modifications. *tier:
  numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/37ed2e63dbb753feb9cda79b08990036/

## Quantum gravity / information paradox / holography  *(speculative context — label, do not let it touch the kernel)*

- **Interior Microstates and Black Hole Entropy** — M. Sasieta, 2026, *Entropy*.
  Review of explicit black-hole interior-microstate constructions in AdS₂
  holography and AdS/CFT, assessing which furnish genuine bases of the black-hole
  Hilbert space; non-perturbative spacetime-wormhole contributions to the
  gravitational path integral resolve the resulting overcounting and reproduce
  the entropy from state counting. *tier: speculative_extension (review).*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/6b9efe17519759b59c76452d16c6de4c/
- **Replica Phase Transition with Quantum Gravity Corrections** — J. Nian et al.,
  2026, *arXiv preprint*. Motivated by bulk replica wormholes, studies the
  boundary effective theory (a Schwarzian mode plus a U(1) phase mode) for
  near-horizon fluctuations of a near-extremal Reissner–Nordström black hole; the
  connected-geometry partition function yields an entropy with a rich phase
  structure, the dominance of connected vs disconnected configurations driving a
  temperature- and coupling-controlled phase transition. *tier:
  speculative_extension.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/190ab3d6149b5fc6ba99ec1437070997/
- **Black hole entropy from the quantum atmosphere of bound gravitational
  fluctuations** — S. Major et al., 2026, *arXiv preprint*. Credible-but-fringe:
  identifies black-hole entropy with counting trapped Regge–Wheeler–Zerilli
  shape-fluctuation modes continually sourced by the Hawking–Unruh process and
  Planck-cutoff-regularized near the horizon; these near-horizon modes store a
  significant fraction of the mass and, unlike quasinormal modes, are not directly
  radiated but perturb near-horizon null rays, with characteristic ~100 Hz
  frequencies for solar-mass holes. *tier: speculative_extension.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/559e27a2f1c351a1b96d0bc2cc242436/
