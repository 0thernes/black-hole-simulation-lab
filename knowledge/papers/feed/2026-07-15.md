## Black-hole shadows / Kerr spin / EHT  *(directly validates the renderer)*

- **Testing non-circular black hole spacetime with X-ray reflection** — L. Gao
  et al., 2026, *European Physical Journal C*. Implements a relativistic
  ray-tracing reflection model in horizon-penetrating (ingoing-Kerr) coordinates
  to test a non-circular metric — a curvature-driven, locality-principle
  deviation from Kerr — against the high-quality NuSTAR spectrum of the Galactic
  binary EXO 1846-031. The fit prefers a high inclination (ι≈76°) and
  near-extremal spin (a*≈0.98); although a global minimum hints at a nonzero
  deformation (ℓ_NP≈0.12), the 99% confidence interval fully encompasses the Kerr
  limit, so current data remain consistent with the Kerr hypothesis while
  establishing a framework for future non-circularity tests. *tier:
  observational_constraint.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/7b803398a46f539095368a257a9a75a4/
- **Signature of iron line profile from a Kerr-like wormhole** — C. Liu et al.,
  2026, *Astronomy & Astrophysics*. Builds a custom ray-tracing reflection
  framework for traversable "Kerr-like" wormhole spacetimes, parameterized by
  spin, throat radius, and shape-function coefficients, and predicts systematically
  narrower Fe Kα lines with suppressed red wings and angle-dependent shifts as the
  throat parameter grows. In synthetic NuSTAR fits, simple convolutional Kerr
  models can mimic a large-throat wormhole with high statistical precision, but a
  self-consistent angle-dependent reflection model fails with structured residuals
  — indicating horizonless throats are degenerate under post-processing yet
  distinguishable with rigid, fully relativistic reflection modeling. *tier:
  numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/3e4ed241bad95a8c95ac3b6ee6c36f3c/
- **The No-Hair Theorems at Work in the Tidal Disruption Event AT2020afhd** —
  L. Iorio, 2026, *Universe*. Models the ~20-day, ~300-day-long coprecession of
  the accretion disk and jet observed in X-ray and radio after the tidal
  disruption event AT2020afhd (a ~10⁷ M_⊙ supermassive black hole) as general
  relativistic Lense-Thirring (gravitomagnetic) precession of an effective test
  orbit; the analytic model yields a dimensionless spin a*≈0.185–0.215 broadly
  consistent with GRMHD estimates and, once the quadrupole moment is included,
  breaks the spin-sign degeneracy — an analytic, precession-based spin probe
  complementary to horizon-scale imaging. *tier: observational_constraint.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/69eeaf73da2b549e89a506a7b3283b97/

## Numerical relativity / ringdown / QNM / self-force  *(adjacent observational GR)*

- **Accuracy of ringdown models calibrated to numerical relativity simulations**
  — 2026, *Physical Review D*. Performs a systematic time-domain mismatch study
  between nonprecessing, quasicircular ringdown models — parametrized by the
  progenitor binary's degrees of freedom — and full inspiral-merger-ringdown
  waveforms from the SXS catalog, to probe whether ringdown descriptions remain
  robust when extrapolated to earlier times. Best-performing models reach
  mismatch ∼[10⁻⁶,10⁻⁴] for the (2,2) harmonic but only ∼[10⁻⁴,10⁻²] for
  higher-order modes, quantifying a modeling gap that must close to meet
  next-generation detector sensitivity in ringdown tests of GR. *tier:
  numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/efbfea2c48405819917255e8015caada/
- **Self-force calculations with numerical relativity methods** — N. L. Vu et al.,
  2026, *arXiv preprint (SpECTRE)*. Introduces a route to generic second-order
  gravitational self-force in Kerr spacetime by importing numerical-relativity
  machinery: an m-mode separation of variables, null ("vtu") slicing in
  horizon-penetrating coordinates, and high-order discontinuous-Galerkin
  discretization with adaptive mesh refinement and a Krylov solver using
  parallel multigrid-Schwarz preconditioning. It achieves exponential convergence
  for the scalar self-force up to spin a=0.998 on circular equatorial orbits down
  to the ISCO (solving 20 m-modes in parallel in seconds), a foundation for
  LISA-quality EMRI waveforms. *tier: numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/445dd8741d945a55b1c33454f9873543/
- **Sensitivity of black hole spectral instability to ultraviolet perturbations**
  — R. Daghigh et al., 2026, *arXiv preprint*. Combines analytic and numerical
  analysis to show the QNM fundamental-mode spectral instability is governed
  primarily by the effective size (integrated strength) of a localized
  effective-potential perturbation rather than its specific shape — persisting
  even as the perturbation width vanishes — while delta-function and
  jump-discontinuity (thin matter shell / Regge-Wheeler) perturbations drive
  competing inward and outward spiral motions of the fundamental frequency; the
  low-lying spectrum is richer than previously anticipated, with implications for
  black-hole spectroscopy in realistic astrophysical environments. *tier:
  numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/6565036e028659fd9ee7d2f1c2b5205b/

## Quantum gravity / information paradox / holography  *(speculative context — label, do not let it touch the kernel)*

- **Quantum Entanglement in the Dirac Field Quantization around Charged Black
  Holes** — A. Chhieb et al., 2026, *Physics Letters B*. Uses concurrence and
  Bures distance to track entanglement of the Dirac field near a
  Reissner-Nordström black hole under Hawking radiation: increasing charge Q
  enhances decoherence inside the horizon while counterintuitively raising the
  accessible entanglement outside, the Hawking effect induces apparent external
  entanglement loss via correlation transfer to inaccessible regions, and
  high-frequency fermionic modes remain resilient near the horizon — a concrete
  map of how curvature and charge redistribute quantum correlations. *tier:
  speculative_extension.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/558f3bf622195e8d8034545cb55e07f3/
- **One-loop aspects of de Sitter axion wormholes** — V. Ivo et al., 2026, *arXiv
  preprint*. Computes, at one loop, the phase of the Euclidean path integral
  around axion-supported de Sitter wormholes and a "multiple wormholes"
  generalization. In the small-handle regime the wormhole admits an effective
  description as a sphere with two local operator insertions whose positions
  fluctuate about an (unstable) antipodal extremum, so the solution's phase is the
  Polchinski sphere phase times an extra phase from the operator-position
  integral; the one-loop determinant also fixes EFT coefficients of dual bilocal
  operators, and "Einstein wormhole" S¹×S^(D-1) solutions are analyzed. *tier:
  speculative_extension.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/72331212e8775bcb8d041c8fc0e43aa1/
- **Quantum simulation of traversable-wormhole-inspired quantum teleportation in a
  chaotic binary sparse SYK model** — M. Byun et al., 2026, *arXiv preprint*.
  Implements the traversable-wormhole teleportation protocol on a quantum
  processor using a chaotic N=8 binary sparse Sachdev-Ye-Kitaev model, with an
  optimized circuit that sharply reduces depth for NISQ hardware while preserving
  the spectral chaos required for gravitational duality. Diagnosed via mutual
  information, the experiment reproduces the essential qualitative signature — a
  sign-dependent teleportation asymmetry — despite NISQ noise precluding exact
  quantitative agreement, establishing a scalable empirical testbed for
  holographic quantum gravity. *tier: speculative_extension.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/5217736803515241a41455ad4ef8da5d/
