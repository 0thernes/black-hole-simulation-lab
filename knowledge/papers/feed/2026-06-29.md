## Black-hole shadows / Kerr spin / EHT  *(directly validates the renderer)*

- **Testing loop quantum gravity through EHT observations of M87* and Sgr A*
  using rotating holonomy-corrected black holes** — Heena Ali et al., 2026,
  *arXiv preprint*. Derives EHT angular-diameter bounds on the LQG holonomy
  parameter b for rotating holonomy-corrected black holes; b enlarges the
  shadow, and closed photon rings persist even without an event horizon, so
  nonzero b stays consistent with current M87*/Sgr A* data. *tier:
  observational_constraint.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/831f23b668915ba6a70bd561b592d285/
- **Impact of Perfect Fluid Dark Matter on the Appearance of Rotating Black
  Hole** — Yu-Xiang Huang et al., 2026, *Physics of the Dark Universe*.
  GR ray-tracing of thin accretion disks at 87/230 GHz around a Kerr black hole
  immersed in perfect-fluid dark matter; ISCO criteria plus EHT angular
  diameters jointly constrain the PFDM intensity k and spin a, with PFDM
  improving agreement with the compact M87* shadow. *tier:
  numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/8255719daced5f0d875f82ab9a1287b9/
- **Rotating Black Holes with Primary Scalar Hair: Shadow Signatures in Beyond
  Horndeski Gravity** — K. Nozari et al., 2026, *arXiv preprint*. A rotating
  primary-scalar-hair configuration in beyond-Horndeski gravity; negative hair
  charge Q enlarges and rounds the shadow while positive Q shrinks and distorts
  it, with EHT θ_d and ΔC bounds carving out the viable (a, Q) region at the
  ~µas sensitivity threshold. *tier: numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/59c3678fb6f4547a818f28e7bdb86684/

## Numerical relativity / ringdown / QNM  *(adjacent observational GR)*

- **Black hole spectroscopy: from theory to experiment** — Emanuele Berti
  et al., 2026, *Classical and Quantum Gravity*. State-of-the-art review of
  quasinormal-mode frequencies, amplitudes, and excitation in GR and modified
  gravity, ringdown waveform modeling, the status of LIGO-Virgo-KAGRA ringdown
  analyses, and prospects for LISA and next-generation detectors. *tier:
  numerical_approximation (review).*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/b83338f1ecc55bf2b6d19437722ddfac/
- **Novel ringdown tests of general relativity with black hole greybody
  factors** — Romeo Felice Rosato et al., 2026, *arXiv preprint*. "GreyRing"
  models the post-merger signal from the remnant greybody factor, reaching
  ~1e-6 (2,2) mismatches against aligned-spin NR waveforms and enabling an
  agnostic strong-gravity consistency test (no overtones needed) that is applied
  to GW250114 and found consistent. *tier: numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/1e675530c91d586c8bb9ee5d2f06a46e/
- **Quasinormal modes and their excitation beyond general relativity. II.
  Isospectrality loss in gravitational waveforms** — Hector O. Silva et al.,
  2026, *Physical Review D*. Time-domain simulations of a Schwarzschild black
  hole in an EFT extension with cubic-in-curvature terms show polar/axial
  isospectrality breaking; identifying the two split fundamental modes from the
  ringdown time series proves generally difficult. *tier:
  numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/3bac03594d925aaf983adbf11e3de08f/

## Quantum gravity / information paradox  *(speculative context — label, do not let it touch the kernel)*

- **Quantum mechanics and observers for gravity in a closed universe** —
  Daniel Harlow et al., 2026, *Journal of High Energy Physics*. Reconciles the
  quantum-extremal-surface result that a closed-universe Hilbert space is
  one-dimensional with lived experience by arguing an observer Ob is
  approximately described by a Hilbert space of dimension ~e^{S_Ob}, with errors
  exponentially small in S_Ob. *tier: speculative_extension.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/55118669be9f5f38ae6171c49fc4893c/
- **Microscopic Origin of Page Curve** — A. Averin, 2026, *arXiv preprint*.
  Uses a generalized Ryu-Takayanagi formula that retains contact with the
  theory's Hamiltonian phase space to argue that phase-space states distinguished
  by Hamiltonian surface charges over the bifurcation surface supply the black
  hole microstates responsible for Wald entropy and the Page curve. *tier:
  speculative_extension.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/22dda35fc5b6528f9deb55b8ecb9e180/
- **Page-curve-like entropy dynamics in a classical elastic bit lattice** —
  A. Faiaz et al., 2026, *Chaos: An Interdisciplinary Journal of Nonlinear
  Science*. Credible-but-fringe analog: four elastically coupled steel spheres
  (two epoxy "elastic bits") reproduce the rise-and-fall Page curve of bipartite
  modal entropy under harmonic driving — a classical correlation analog, not a
  model of Hawking radiation or quantum gravity. *tier: speculative_extension.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/d4e109107285575caf068e7a502669c9/
