## Black-hole shadows / Kerr spin / EHT  *(directly validates the renderer)*

- **EHT Observational Constraints on Dymnikova-Type Non-Singular Black Holes in
  Higher Dimensions** — A. Errehymy et al., 2026, *Physics Letters B*. Extends the
  Dymnikova regular black hole (de Sitter-like core, two horizons) to higher
  dimensions via a smooth matter distribution: circular photon orbits stay
  unstable so a well-defined shadow persists, the shadow grows with the black-hole
  scale but shrinks slightly with added dimensions, and Hawking temperature and
  energy emission depend strongly on dimensionality. Comparison with EHT M87*/Sgr
  A* constrains the model parameters. *tier: observational_constraint.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/d1febaa253755ad3ba0984448095260a/
- **Polarization Signatures from GRMHD Simulations of Black Hole Accretion** —
  P. C. Fragile et al., 2026, *book chapter*. A survey of the still-young effort to
  extract polarization signatures directly from general-relativistic MHD
  simulations of accretion onto compact objects, contrasting the abundant
  phenomenological models with the scarce first-principles simulation results, and
  arguing that pairing X-ray (and radio, à la EHT) polarimetry with accretion
  simulations is the route to constraining disk, corona and jet properties near the
  horizon. *tier: numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/5b938e1a4f045402b6acad529b9acbe4/

## Numerical relativity / ringdown / QNM / GW modeling  *(adjacent observational GR)*

- **Quasinormal modes of four-dimensional regular black holes in quasi-topological
  gravity: overtones' outburst via the WKB method** — R. Konoplya, 2026, *Physics
  Letters B*. Scalar, electromagnetic and Dirac QNMs of regular black holes in
  non-polynomial quasi-topological gravity: while the fundamental mode varies
  smoothly with the regularization parameter, higher overtones show markedly
  enhanced sensitivity to near-horizon modifications (the "outburst of overtones"),
  and high-order WKB with Padé resummation — cross-checked against time-domain and
  Leaver methods — already detects the onset, making overtone dynamics a sensitive
  probe of geometric regularity. *tier: numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/eb632fbe7dc75530a9664c437f16975a/
- **The Quadratic Quasi-Normal Modes of a Schwarzschild Black Hole** —
  B. Bucciotti et al., 2026, *Journal of Physics: Conference Series*. A perturbative
  framework yielding quadratic-QNM (QQNM) amplitudes and phases for arbitrary
  angular momentum, parity and overtone number in Schwarzschild: the ratio of a
  quadratic mode to its linear parents is a universal prediction of perturbation
  theory, independent of the initial data up to a known parity dependence and in
  some cases large enough to be detectable, reproducing known results and
  predicting new quadratic modes of observational interest. *tier:
  numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/38db396feb7f5126af1a916cce1b1f28/
- **Exact WKB and Quantum Periods for Extremal Black Hole Quasinormal Modes** —
  Y. Hatsuda et al., 2026, *arXiv preprint*. Applies exact WKB analysis to
  black-hole perturbation theory: the QNM boundary conditions become exact
  quantization conditions on the complex frequencies, solved by evaluating quantum
  periods (Voros symbols) to very high WKB order with Borel–Padé resummation. For
  scalar perturbations of extremal Reissner–Nordström and Kerr black holes the
  resummed conditions reproduce the correct QNM frequencies to high precision.
  *tier: numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/18c8b8903e7255f3b5fcea5a12b51c1b/
- **Probing beyond-vacuum general relativistic effects with extreme mass-ratio
  inspirals** — T. Zi et al., 2026, *arXiv preprint*. Treats EMRIs as probes of
  both astrophysical environments (a cold-dark-matter halo modifying the GW flux
  and adding dynamical friction) and scalar-Gauss–Bonnet gravity (an effective
  scalar charge sourcing scalar radiation): a two-timescale, fixed-frequency
  analysis gives leading-order flux corrections for quasi-circular equatorial
  orbits, and a Fisher-matrix study quantifies whether LISA can disentangle
  environmental from modified-gravity imprints in the accumulated dephasing and
  waveform mismatch. *tier: numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/ac53c551f3cb5fbda80b8c350d9834d0/

## Quantum gravity / information paradox / holography  *(speculative context — label, do not let it touch the kernel)*

- **Black holes from the gravitational path integral: supersymmetric indices and
  precision holography** — E. Turetta, 2026, *arXiv preprint (thesis)*. Formulates
  the protected supersymmetric index that counts SUSY black-hole microstates
  directly in the gravitational Euclidean path integral, where in the semiclassical
  limit it reduces to a sum over complex non-extremal saddles in both AdS and flat
  space; four-derivative corrections to AdS₅ black-hole thermodynamics match a
  Cardy-like limit of the dual superconformal index, and a broad new family of
  asymptotically-flat saddles (multi-horizon, lens-space bubbles, spindles) is
  classified by rod structure. *tier: speculative_extension.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/823a0b1b37625b18a7ca03cd7076c67a/
- **Hawking Atmosphere of Anti-de Sitter Black Holes** — A. Cardona et al., 2026,
  *Universe*. Models the semiclassical evaporation of spherically symmetric AdS
  black holes via Parikh–Wilczek tunneling with backreaction (emission probability
  tied to the Bekenstein–Hawking entropy change), finding the time-dependent
  luminosity deviates strongly from blackbody for small AdS holes — where luminosity
  does not rise with temperature because of large mass variation — and complements
  this with the renormalized stress tensor of a quantum field in the evaporating
  Vaidya-AdS geometry. *tier: numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/80e77c51cda75c168abbf8cfe2624f8e/
- **The fate of Reissner–Nordström–de Sitter black holes: nonequilibrium discharge
  and evaporation** — D. Easson, 2026, *arXiv preprint*. A semiclassical RN–dS
  evaporation model combining 2D dilaton gravity with Polyakov-anomaly backreaction
  yields closed adiabatic evolution equations for mass and charge: neutral
  Schwarzschild–de Sitter holes lose mass monotonically along the sub-Nariai
  branch, Schwinger pair-production drives rapid discharge, and the classical
  cold/extremal, charged-Nariai, ultracold and lukewarm loci are shown *not* to be
  semiclassical attractors — the endpoint is empty de Sitter, with the analysis
  framing conservative quantum-extremal-surface/island estimates. *tier:
  numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/fb172197e2d053699dc1d29438f6a7f6/
