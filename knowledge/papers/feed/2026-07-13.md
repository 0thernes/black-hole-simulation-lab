## Black-hole shadows / Kerr spin / EHT  *(directly validates the renderer)*

- **Unveiling Inner Shadows and Polarization Signatures of Rotating
  Einstein–Gauss–Bonnet Black Holes** — B.-b. Chen et al., 2026, *Chinese Physics
  C*. Backward ray-tracing of a rotating EGB black hole with a thin accretion
  disk: the Gauss–Bonnet coupling ξ mainly sets the size of the inner shadow while
  spin controls its deformation, the photon-ring morphology is more sensitive to
  viewing inclination than to ξ, and the linear-polarization direction near the
  inner shadow and photon ring responds clearly to ξ — arguing that combining
  total-intensity and polarization images discriminates such objects better than
  either alone. *tier: numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/1a0abd1b46f9541b8727e454fb6c2131/
- **Gravitational lensing and observational features of a dynamic black hole** —
  K.-J. He et al., 2026, *arXiv preprint*. Backward ray-tracing of a Vaidya
  (accreting, time-dependent) black hole: the shadow evolves from an initial
  static configuration through expansion to a new static state, a lensing ring
  appears outside the shadow only in the quiescent phases, and a novel
  dynamical-redshift ring emerges and brightens as accretion proceeds — offering a
  time-domain observable intrinsic to evolving spacetimes. *tier:
  numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/8b4a1892787b55d0becb84b4ea076e95/
- **Shadow of a Rotating Black Hole Immersed in a Dark Matter Halo** — Y.-Y. Liu
  et al., 2026, *arXiv preprint*. Builds a Kerr-like metric with a
  double-power-law dark-matter-modified mass function via the Newman–Janis
  algorithm: the halo enlarges the horizon, pushes the photon shell outward and
  modifies the shadow (more strongly for rapid spin), and the Sgr A* shadow
  measurement is used to constrain the halo parameter space. *tier:
  observational_constraint.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/2b5fed29d60c57c6b882759165fe2f85/

## Numerical relativity / ringdown / QNM / GW modeling  *(adjacent observational GR)*

- **Impact of waveform systematics and Gaussian noise on the interpretation of
  GW231123** — S. Bini et al., 2026, *Physical Review D*. GW231123 (the most
  massive LVK binary to date, ~250 M☉) shows large model-to-model differences over
  its few observed cycles; using the NRSur7dq4 numerical-relativity surrogate the
  authors reproduce those systematics on simulated data and show the headline
  properties — high component masses, high spin magnitudes (median χ₁ ≳ 0.7), high
  precession — are robust against waveform systematics, noise realizations, and
  single-detector inference. *tier: observational_constraint.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/c386a4f0cbfd5098bfce4d7ea22e6c33/
- **An autoencoder-based surrogate waveform model for quasi-circular
  binary-black-hole mergers** — A. Theodoropoulos et al., 2026, *arXiv preprint*.
  AESur3dq8 is an autoencoder generative model trained on the NR-informed
  NRHybSur3dq8 surrogate and fine-tuned on the SXS catalog: it builds millions of
  quasi-circular BBH waveforms in under a second on modest hardware, reaches
  mismatches ~10⁻⁴ against numerical relativity, and yields parameter estimates
  consistent with LVK results. *tier: numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/8b30c33578605a248f9c146a3daba180/
- **Merger remnant and eccentricity dynamics surrogates for eccentric nonspinning
  black hole binaries** — A. Ravichandran et al., 2026, *arXiv preprint*. Two
  NR-trained models for unequal-mass (q ≤ 4), non-spinning, eccentric (e < 0.23)
  BBHs: one predicts remnant mass, spin and recoil, the other is a time-domain
  surrogate for eccentricity and mean-anomaly evolution — quantifying how residual
  eccentricity imprints on remnant properties (relevant to precision ringdown
  tests and hierarchical-merger modeling) and enhances or suppresses recoil.
  *tier: numerical_approximation.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/178d41f57bc0588a95ea689be126b30e/

## Quantum gravity / information paradox / holography  *(speculative context — label, do not let it touch the kernel)*

- **Chaos of Berry curvature for BPS microstates** — Y. Chen et al., 2026, *arXiv
  preprint*. Proposes the non-Abelian Berry curvature under coupling deformations
  as an intrinsic chaos diagnostic for supersymmetric black-hole microstates
  (whose exactly degenerate levels defeat ordinary level statistics): for
  horizonless 1/2- and 1/4-BPS states the Berry curvature is non-random or exactly
  zero, while for states dual to SUSY black holes (via 𝒩=2 super-JT and the 𝒩=2
  SYK model) it resembles a random matrix — sharply separating black-hole
  microstates from smooth geometries. *tier: speculative_extension.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/e0fc5cdf1fe25039a2eaebb5aec31568/
- **Trade-off Relation for Black Hole Entropy Fluctuations** — K.
  Gallock-Yoshimura et al., 2026, *arXiv preprint*. Within stochastic
  semiclassical gravity, derives a trade-off between the variance of a black
  hole's entropy change and the which-path information carried by infalling
  quantum matter (photons in the Danielson–Satishchandran–Wald decoherence
  experiment): a horizon cannot record relevant quantum information with
  arbitrarily small entropy fluctuations. *tier: speculative_extension.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/0970b6e9d581556f81da90241c166863/
- **Quantizing the Exterior Region of a Schwarzschild–AdS Black Hole Leads to a
  Resolution of the Information Paradox on a Quantum Level** — C. Gerhardt, 2026,
  *Symmetry*. (Credible-but-fringe, single-author programme.) Quantizes the
  exterior Schwarzschild–AdS region in the author's canonical quantum-gravity
  model, choosing interior-matched eigenvalue multiplicities to define a unitary
  equivalence between the interior and exterior Hilbert spaces and Hamiltonians,
  and argues this leaves no information paradox at the quantum level. *tier:
  speculative_extension.*
  https://consensus.app/papers/details/dcc2ae5f5c6d5be0be7a28a5e07798e2/
